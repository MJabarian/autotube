Story:
[curious] You won't believe these massive geoglyphs in Peru! The Nazca Lines cover 500 square kilometers, depicting creatures like monkeys and hummingbirds. Created between 500 BCE and 500 CE, their purpose is a mystery. Some experts believe these lines served as an astronomical calendar to track celestial events, crucial for agricultural cycles. Others argue they were part of ancient rituals. Over 70 years after their discovery, we’re still searching for answers—who created them, and why? This unsolved puzzle is mind-blowing!
