[excited] You won’t believe how togas defined ancient Roman life! In 44 BC, Julius Caesar turned heads in a striking red toga, symbolizing his power. But did you know toga colors revealed your social status? The plain white toga signified a citizen, while the purple-bordered toga was reserved for senators! Plus, victorious generals flaunted a special crimson toga picta during parades. This colorful fashion spoke volumes about politics and prestige. So, next time you think of a toga, remember—it's more than just draped fabric—it's a story of ambition and identity!
